2.5.2. Flower Shape¶

2.5.2.1. Problem Description¶

This example solves Poisson’s equation

\[\nabla \cdot \beta \nabla \phi = 7 r^2 cos 3 \theta\]

where, \(\beta = 1\) in present case.

The domain is defined by

\[\Omega = {(r, \theta) : r \le 0.30 + 0.15 cos 6 \theta}\]

Dirichlet boundary conditions on \(\partial \Omega\) are specified by the exact solution,

\[\varphi (r, \theta) = r^4 cos3 \theta\]

2.5.2.2. Error Definition¶

Volume-weighted norm of a variable \(e\),

\[||e||^{\Lambda, \Omega_k}_{p} = (\sum_{\Omega_k} |e|^p \Lambda V)^{1/p}\]

where \(V = \Delta x \Delta y\) is the cell volume. \(\Lambda\) is volume fraction for cut cell.

The rate of convergence between two norms, \(e_1\) and \(e_2\), with two different grid spacings \(h_1\) and \(h2\), as

\[r = log(\frac{e_1}{e_2}) / log(\frac{h_1}{h_2})\]

Thus with \(h_1 < h_2\), a rate of \(r=1\) for the two errors \(e_1\) and \(e_2\) indicates a first-order accurate method.
