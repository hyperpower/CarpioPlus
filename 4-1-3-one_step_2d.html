2.4.3. One Step 2D¶

2.4.3.1. Problem Description¶

The domain is a unit square \(x \in [0.0, 1.0], y \in [0.0, 1.0]\). The boudary conditions are

\[\begin{split}\phi = 1 &, \quad \text{if} \quad x = 0.0\\ \frac{d \phi}{d x} = 0 &, \quad \text{if} \quad x = 1.0\\ \phi = 0 &, \quad \text{if} \quad y = 0.0\\ \frac{d \phi}{d y} = 0 &, \quad \text{if} \quad y = 1.0\\\end{split}\]

The velocity is \(\mathbf{u}=(1, 1)\). The final profile will be \(\phi = 1.0\) on left top of the domain. Meanwhile, the right bottom profile will be \(\phi = 0.0\).

Fig. 2.34 Illustration of the problem.¶

The \(y=x\) is a step which jumps from 1 to 0.

2.4.3.2. Results¶

2.4.3.2.1. First Order Upwind¶

Initial condition of the domain is \(\phi = 0.0\). The iteration stops when \(||\phi^{n+1} - \phi^{n}|| < 10^-4\). CFL number is 0.5.
