2.1.3.2.4. Line vs Box - Positive Side¶

A line cuts a box into two sides. Here, we get a positive side as a ploygon.

We want to construct a line rotate about a point, say p1: \((x1, y1)\). p1 is the rotational center. And, we have another point p2: math:(x2, y2). A line can be constructed in two points form.

Given two different points \((x1, y1)\) and \((x2, y2)\), there is exactly one line that passes through them. There are several ways to write a linear equation of this line.

If \(x1 ≠ x2\), the slope of the line is \(\frac{y_{2}-y_{1}}{x_{2}-x_{1}}\) Thus, a point-slope form is

\[\begin{split}y - y_{1} &= \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} (x - x_1) \\ (x_2 - x_1)(y - y_1) &= (y_2 - y_1)(x - x_1)\end{split}\]

finally, the line equation will be

\[(y_1 - y_2) x + (x_2 - x_1) y + (x_1 y_2 - x_2 y_1) = 0\]

p2 is also a point on a circle. The center of the circle is p1. Radius of this circle is r. Thus, p2 can be obtained by

\[\begin{split}x_2 &= x_1 + r \cos \theta \\ y_2 &= y_1 + r \sin \theta\end{split}\]
